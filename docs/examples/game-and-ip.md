## Game CG teaser

### A souls-like action game

> A souls-like action-game CG. Inside a burning Gothic ruined cathedral, ash drifts down from the sky. At the center of the frame, a five-meter-tall heavily-armored boss slowly draws a great sword that burns with crimson flame from where it was driven into the ground. Cold blue light leaks from the seams of the boss's helmet. The camera looks up from low at the boss's feet, then rapidly orbits around it. Top-tier Unreal Engine 5 (UE5) render quality, epic-scale oppressiveness, dark fantasy.

### An open-world game

> An ancient ruin reawakens from its slumber; the massive stone walls crack open slowly, dust, snow, and ancient runes all stirred awake together. A lone character stands at the edge of the ruins, cloak lifted by the wind, as if having just arrived at the end of the world — and also as if about to step into a long-predestined fate. The distant sky presses very low; mountains, broken walls, a fractured god-statue, and the lighting-up ruin core together form a vast, dangerous opening for the world. The teaser is in no rush to explain the backstory — it throws out epic scale, fatefulness, and unknown threat all at once, so you feel this world has existed a very long time, and the real story is only just beginning.

## World-building atmosphere

### Building a future world

> An enormous sky-city built above a sea of clouds slowly wakes in the quietest moment before sunrise. Towering spires, floating bridges, and translucent energy rails are still wrapped in faint blue morning mist; a low, regular mechanical rumble comes from afar. At the city's edge, a delivery child rides a small flying craft through layer after layer of towers, past windows just lighting up, past colossal statues and temple ruins not yet awake. As the sun rises bit by bit, the whole city shifts from cold, mysterious quiet into busy, magnificent, order-filled civilizational life. The point of the frame isn't any single character — it's to make the viewer believe: this world really exists, and it has its own history, rules, and breathing.

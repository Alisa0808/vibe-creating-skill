# Literary visualization

Literary expression already carries strong imagery, emotion, and narrative tension; Vibe Creating turns that text into footage naturally. The pieces below are **original paraphrases** in the spirit of common literary moods — not quotations of real works.

## Wordless relationships

### A winter-night stove

> Once it gets dark, only the fire in the stove still glows. Grandma sits feeding kindling, the firelight throwing her wrinkles into shifting light and shadow, like cracked field ridges. Radish and pork ribs stew in the pot; the steam keeps nudging the lid into a soft rattle. Her little grandson lies nearby doing homework — and falls asleep mid-sentence, forehead pressed to the notebook, pencil still clutched in his hand.

<details><summary>原文</summary>

> 天一黑，屋里就只剩灶膛的火还亮着。奶奶坐在灶前添柴，火光把她脸上的皱纹照得一深一浅，像干裂的田埂。锅里炖着萝卜和排骨，热气顶得锅盖轻轻作响。小孙子趴在一旁写作业，写着写着睡着了，额头压在本子上，铅笔还攥在手里。

</details>

### Grinding beans at dawn

> The sky is just turning pale; mist hasn't lifted off the bluestone street. The smell of soybeans seeps into the cold air with the creak of the stone mill. She pushes it round and round, her thin figure stubborn before the millstone. The stall is gone, but the work must go on. A street-sweeping man comes up the alley, sets down his broom, and silently grips the other end of the mill handle. No words — only the mill grinding heavily in the morning light, as if resisting some wordless fate.

<details><summary>原文</summary>

> 天刚泛白，青石街雾气未散。豆香随石磨吱呀声渗入冷空气。她一圈一圈推着，单薄身影在磨盘前显得执拗。摊子没了，活计还得续。巷口走来个扫地男人，放下扫帚，默不作声握住磨杆另一头。两人无言，只有石磨在晨光里沉重咬合，像是在对抗某种无声的命数。

</details>

## Atmosphere / group scenes

### The courtyard after the autumn harvest

> The harvest is just over; corn husks still pile in the yard, not yet brought inside. When the evening wind rises, scraps of leaf and dust roll slowly along the base of the wall. An old man crouches by the threshold mending an old bamboo basket, the veins on his hands standing out one by one, like the roots of the old pagoda tree behind the yard. No one speaks indoors — only the water boiling on the stove, the lid hopping up and down.

<details><summary>原文</summary>

> 秋收刚过，院子里还堆着没来得及收进屋的玉米皮。傍晚的风一起，碎叶和尘土就沿着墙根慢慢滚。老人蹲在门槛边修一只旧竹筐，手上的青筋一根根鼓出来，像院子后头那棵老槐树的根。屋里没有人说话，只有灶上水开了，壶盖一下一下地跳。

</details>

### When the market breaks up

> As dusk falls, the market crowd thins out wave by wave. The tofu seller pushes his plank cart into the alley; the cloth-seller woman gathers in her hanging floral fabrics; a gust lifts a few scraps of old newspaper off the ground. A child carrying a just-bought sugar figurine hops along chasing the grandmother ahead, nearly kicking over a bamboo basket of greens by the road. Somewhere far off, a last "Closing up!" — and the whole street slowly folds the daytime away.

<details><summary>原文</summary>

> 天快黑时，集市上的人一拨拨散了。卖豆腐的把木板车往巷子里推，卖布的女人收起晾着的花布，风一吹，几片废报纸从地上卷起来。一个小孩提着刚买的糖人，一路跳着去追走在前面的奶奶，差点踩翻路边一只装着青菜的竹篮。远处有人吆喝最后一声"收摊喽"，整条街像慢慢把白天合上。

</details>

## Multi-scene journey

### Following a character through shifting spaces

> The day she enters the city, a fine rain is falling; the gray-brick walls are wet to black. Carrying a heavy leather case, she hesitates before the great vermilion door, looks up — the deep mansion's gray walls rise oppressively high. Alone she crosses courtyard after courtyard, her footsteps echoing lonely down the cloister, the lanterns on both sides all dark. She pushes open the innermost door: the room is pitch black, only a single unlit candle stub on the windowsill — like the coldest footnote this house could leave.

<details><summary>原文</summary>

> 进城那天落着细雨，青砖墙湿得发黑。她提着沉重皮箱，在那道朱漆大门前迟疑，抬头望去，深宅灰墙高得压人。她独自穿过重重庭院，脚步声在回廊孤零零回响，两侧灯笼全灭着。推开最深处那扇房门，屋里黑沉沉的，只有窗台摆着一支没点火的残烛，像是这个家最冷清的注脚。

</details>

## Memory flashback

### A whirling-lantern recollection

> In a daze, he seems to return to that opulent noon. Still the boy in a silk-brocade jacket, riding to school on the broad back of an old servant, insisting on a stick of bright-red candied haws as they pass the market. A scolding comes from behind; mischievously, he ignores it. The shot shifts — and he is bent over in cracked field furrows, a fistful of sweat-smelling wet mud in his hand, the scorching sun searing the back of his neck. Those bygone days feel like another person's distant dream.

<details><summary>原文</summary>

> 恍惚间，他好像又回到了那个锦衣玉食的响午。还是那个穿着绸缎马褂的少年，骑在老仆宽背上进学，路过街市非要吃红艳艳的糖葫芦。训斥声从后头传来，他却调皮地不理。镜头一晃，他正弓腰蹲在干裂田垄间，手里攥着一把带汗味的湿泥，火辣辣的日头把后颈晒得生疼。那些往事，仿佛是另一个人的遥远梦境。

</details>

## Classical imagery

Vivid proof that even sparse classical-style language carries strong feeling — and that the model reads the historical and emotional subtext, not just the surface scene.

### Snow, in the spirit of a Ming-dynasty lake pavilion

> A heavy snow has fallen for three days; on the lake, the sounds of people and birds are gone. Rime hangs thick — sky and cloud and hill and water are one expanse of white. The only shadows on the lake: a single streak of the long causeway, one dot of the mid-lake pavilion, one mustard-seed of my boat, and two or three grains of people aboard it.

<details><summary>原文（仿写，取意于古典写雪的清寂）</summary>

> 大雪三日，湖中人鸟声俱绝。雾凇沆砀，天与云与山与水，上下一白。湖上影子，惟长堤一痕、湖心亭一点、与余舟一芥、舟中人两三粒而已。

</details>
